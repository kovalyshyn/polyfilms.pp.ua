DarkroomLog

Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 29, 2026

Overview

DarkroomLog is an iOS app for analog darkroom photographers. This policy explains what data the app stores and how it is handled.

Data We Collect

DarkroomLog stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Session names, dates, and notes you enter manually
  • Equipment names (enlarger, lens, paper, developer) you add to the library
  • Exposure times and notes for individual prints
  • Photos of prints you choose to attach from your photo library

Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect any personal information
  • We do not use analytics or tracking
  • We do not share any data with third parties
  • We do not have access to your data — it never leaves your device

Photo Library Access

DarkroomLog uses the system photo picker (PhotosPicker) provided by iOS. This means the app never receives direct access to your photo library. You choose a specific photo to attach, and only that photo's data is stored locally in the app.

iCloud Backup

App data is included in your standard iOS iCloud Backup if you have it enabled in your device settings. This backup is managed entirely by Apple and is subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all stored data.

Children's Privacy

DarkroomLog does not collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date.

Contact
